{\footnotesize\ttfamily static double moa.\+gui.\+experimentertab.\+statisticaltests.\+C\+D\+F\+\_\+\+Normal.\+xnormi (\begin{DoxyParamCaption}\item[{double}]{p }\end{DoxyParamCaption})\hspace{0.3cm}{\ttfamily [static]}}

This method calculates the normal cdf inverse function. 

Let P\+H\+I(x) be the normal cdf. Suppose that Q calculates 1.\+0 -\/ P\+H\+I(x), and that Q\+I\+NV calculates Q\+I\+N\+V(p) for p in (0.\+0,.5\mbox{]}. Then for p .le. .5, x = P\+H\+I\+I\+N\+V(p) = -\/Q\+I\+N\+V(p). For p .gt. .5, x = P\+H\+I\+I\+N\+V(p) = Q\+I\+NV(1.\+0 -\/ p). The formula for approximating Q\+I\+NV is taken from Abramowitz and Stegun, Handbook of Mathematical Functions, Dover, 9th printing, formula 26.\+2.\+3, page 933. The error in x is claimed to be less than 4.\+5e-\/4 in absolute value.
