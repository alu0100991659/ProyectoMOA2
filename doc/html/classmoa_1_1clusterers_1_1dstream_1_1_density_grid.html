Detailed Description

Density Grids are defined in equation 3 (section 3.1) of Chen and Tu 2007 as: In D-Stream, we partition the d−dimensional space S into density grids. Suppose for each dimension, its space Si, i =1, ··· ,d is divided into pi partitions as Si = Si,1 U Si,2 U ··· U Si,pi (2) then the data space S is partitioned into N = PRODUCT (i=1..d) pi density grids. For a density grid g that is composed of S1,j1 ×S2,j2 ···×Sd,jd , ji =1, ...,pi, we denote it as g =(j1,j2, ··· ,jd). (3)

Citation: Y. Chen and L. Tu, “Density-Based Clustering for Real-Time Stream Data,” in Proceedings of the 13th ACM SIGKDD international conference on Knowledge discovery and data mining, 2007, pp. 133–142.
